"Professor, what is the actual difference between a wealthy person and a truly wise person?[span_4](start_span)"[span_4](end_span)

"Well, Tiger, a wealthy person simply possesses a massive amount of currency[span_5](start_span)[span_5](end_span). For instance, take my wealthy cousin[span_6](start_span)[span_6](end_span). He is undeniably rich[span_7](start_span)[span_7](end_span). He owns a Pajero and a Prado[span_8](start_span)[span_8](end_span). He spends his entire life traveling from one auction to another, using the sheer weight of his money to buy everything up and resell it for a massive profit[span_11](start_span)[span_11](end_span). That is what a rich man is—someone whose car is overflowing with stacked bundles of 1 million, 10 million, or 100 million kyats[span_12](start_span)[span_12](end_span). He is drowning in material currency[span_13](start_span)[span_13](end_span)."
### 🐯 Tiger
"Professor, can I pray and request to the Buddha everyday so that I can possess bags of cash just like that wealthy cousin?[span_14](start_span)"[span_14](end_span)
### 🧙‍♂️ The Professor
"If you want to pray, you must practice and cultivate your mind accordingly[span_15](start_span)[span_15](end_span). But consider this: you are asking for money from the Buddha, who completely detached Himself from currency and worldly wealth[span_16](start_span)[span_16](end_span). You are asking for gold from a teacher who dispenses pure wisdom instead of material riches[span_17](start_span)[span_17](end_span). 
If your true goal is simply to be rich, then save your money[span_18](start_span)[span_18](end_span). Once you become rich, you can choose to give[span_19](start_span)[span_19](end_span). But if you have an immense, burning desire to give back to the world while you are still poor, then do exactly as I do[span_20](start_span)[span_20](end_span). 
Even though I am a devout Buddhist, on Saturdays and Sundays, I cross over to other places of worship to cook for their congregants[span_21](start_span)[span_21](end_span). Before leaving, those worshippers enjoy the warm sago dessert I prepared, and they quietly drop their small, tiny coins into the collection bowl near the entrance[span_22](start_span)[span_22](end_span). At the end of the day, the priest hands over those gathered coins to me[span_23](start_span)[span_23](end_span). 
Do you know what I achieved with those tiny coins, Tiger[span_24](start_span)[span_24](end_span)? With that exact money, I bought international plane tickets for my people[span_25](start_span)[span_25](end_span). I extended my charity so far that I was able to purchase flight tickets for the families of my closest friends[span_26](start_span)[span_26](end_span). Today, those children have grown into sophisticated ladies living independently abroad—they probably don’t even know how their flight tickets were paid for or where that money came from[span_27](start_span)[span_27](end_span). 
In this world, a true helper must never let the desire for recognition, fame, or status come first[span_28](start_span)[span_28](end_span). It is always about how we can quietly, selflessly serve others[span_29](start_span)[span_29](end_span). Even if you possess absolutely nothing and have no specialized technical skills, if you have humility alone, you can still practice the most profound charity[span_30](start_span)[span_30](end_span). With just these tiny, overlooked coins, I can give back to the world much more than my wealthy cousin ever does with his cars and auctions[span_31](start_span)[span_31](end_span)."

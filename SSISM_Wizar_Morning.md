Morning Reflection

> The world does not merely need more people who speak.
It needs more people who quietly repair.



There are many kinds of work.

Some build buildings.

Some write software.

Some heal the sick.

Some teach children.

Some collect rubbish from the streets.

Some repair broken bridges.

Some repair broken trust.

All are forms of caring for the world.

A civilization is strengthened not only by great leaders, but also by countless ordinary people who willingly repair what is broken.

Today's Classroom

The Professor enters carrying only a broom.

The students laugh.

"Professor...

Where is today's lesson?"

The Professor smiles.

"I brought today's lesson."

He gently sweeps the classroom floor.

One student asks,

"Professor... why are you cleaning?"

The Professor replies,

> "Every generation likes building new things.



> Few volunteer to repair what already exists.



> Today we begin there."



The classroom becomes silent.

Today's Civic Reflection

Burmese reflection can be expressed as a universal civic message:

> A healthy society needs people who protect, maintain, repair, and care for the common good—even when no one is watching and even when there is no reward.



That is a message that speaks not only to Burma but to any community.
